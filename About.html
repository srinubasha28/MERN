About events

1857 :

The Indian Rebellion of 1857, also known as the Sepoy Mutiny, takes place in India against British colonial rule.

The first railway in India is opened, connecting Bombay to Thane.

1858 :

The United States and Japan sign the Harris Treaty, a treaty of amity and commerce.

The British North America Act creates the Dominion of Canada, a self-governing entity within the British Empire.

1885 :

The Berlin Conference establishes the principle of effective occupation and the concept of spheres of influence, which becomes a cornerstone of international law.

The African Society of Nigeria is founded.

1906 :

The Wright brothers, Orville and Wilbur Wright, successfully fly the world's first powered, heavier-than-air aircraft at Kitty Hawk, North Carolina.

The International Conference of Rome establishes the Ottoman Empire's territorial integrity.

1920 :

The League of Nations is established as an international organization to promote peace and security after World War I.

The Nineteenth Amendment to the United States Constitution grants women's suffrage.

1930 :

The British Empire's population reaches its peak at over 450 million people.

The construction of the Hoover Dam begins in the United States.

1942 :

The United States begins Lend-Lease aid to Great Britain during World War II.

The Wannsee Conference takes place in Germany, where high-ranking Nazi officials discuss and plan the "Final Solution" for the Holocaust.

1947 :

India and Pakistan gain independence from British colonial rule through partition.

Israel declares independence from British colonial rule.
